Dr. Magdi Yacoub

Egyptian heart surgeon

Dr. Magdi H. Yacoub, Professor of Cardiothoracic Surgery at the National Heart and Lung Institute, Imperial College London, Director of Research at the Magdi Yacoub Institute at Harefield Heart Science Centre, President of the Chain of Hope and Director of the Magdi Yacoub Heart Foundation

Professor Sir Magdi Yacoub is Professor of Cardiothoracic Surgery at the National Heart and Lung Institute, Imperial College London, Director of Research at the Magdi Yacoub Institute at Harefield Heart Science Centre, President of the Chain of Hope and Director of the Magdi Yacoub Heart Foundation which created the Aswan Heart Centre.

Research led by Professor Sir Magdi Yacoub include tissue engineering heart valves, myocardial regeneration, novel left ventricular assist devices and wireless sensors with collaborations within Imperial College, nationally and internationally.

His Centre in Aswan offers state-of-the art medical services free of charge to all patients regardless of colour, religion, or gender and trains a generation of young Egyptian doctors, nurses, scientists, and technicians at the highest international standards.

Here's a time line of Dr. Yacoub's life:

  • 1935 - Born in Belbis, Ashraqya, Egypt
  • 1957 - Studied at Cairo University and qualified as a doctor
  • 1962 - He moved to Britain, then taught at the University of Chicago.
  • 1974 - He performed the first open heart surgery in Nigeria
  • 1983 - Performed the UK's first heart and lung transplant at Harefield
  • 1984 - Magdi Yacoub First Heart transplant on newborn baby
  • 1988 - Bradshaw Lecture, Royal College of Physicians
  • 1992 - Sir Magdi was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II
  • 1995 - Founded the UK charity Chain of Hope
  • 2001 - Magdi retired from his work in London-area hospitals and spends his time recruiting doctors from around the world for the UK's National Health Services. He has also devoted significant time to his charity Chain of Hope
  • 2006 - European Society of Cardiology Gold Medal
  • 2008 - He established the Magdi Yacoub Heart Foundation
  • 2011 - Order of the Nile for science and humanity
  • 2014 - Professor Sir Magdi Yacoub received the UK Order of Merit from Queen Elizabeth II, becoming the first Egyptian to ever receive the prestigious award
